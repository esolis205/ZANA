Vitamin A

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Eye Health and Vision
  • Immune System
  • Reproduction
  • Fetal Development

The active form which is found in Vitamin A shields and guards both the cornea, and conjuctiva of the eye.

Important Fact

Vitamin A gives us humans the ability to see in color, suprsingly enough it also gives us the ability to see in low light.

Similar To

  • Carotenoids

A molecule that performs the same task's as Vitamin A. Also, they act as pigments, which give red, orange, and yellow foods their color. The body takes Carotenoids and converts them to Vitamine A , retinol. The most common form of Carotenoid is Beta-carotene.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Dry Skin or Rash
  • Frequent Infections
  • Night Blindness or Impaired Vision
  • In developing countries, Xerophthalmia (Thickness and Dryness of the cornea)

Overdose Symptoms

  • Nausea/Vomiting
  • Yellowed or Severely dry skin
  • Bone Pain
  • Vision Changes
  • Swelling of the bones

  • Halibut

Vitamin D (Calciferol)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Growth and Development
  • Absorption and Metabolism of Calcium
  • Absorption and Metabolism of Phosphorus
  • Supports Hardening of Bones and Teeth

Vitamin D is involved with Calcium and Phosphorus metabolism, helping your body better absorp them.

Important Fact

Vitamin D has shown to improve muscle strength, immune function maintnance, and reduces the risk of Osteoporosis, also involved in cell division processes.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Soft Bones
  • Bowed Legs
  • Poor Teeth
  • Skeletal Deformities
  • Rickets (Severe deformation of the skeleton, found in children).
  • Loss of bone density and increased fractures (in adults).

Consider the Following

Elderly individuals who spent most of their time indoors should consider supplementation.

Overdose Symptoms

  • Calcium build-up in soft tissues.
  • Irreversible Kidney Damage
  • Irreversible Cardiovascular Damage
  • Vision Changes
  • Swelling of the bones
Warning
  • Overconsumption of Vitamine D can be harmful to children.
  • Utilization of 100mcg or more is considered overdose.
  • Areas that experience sunlight limitations, or individuals who receive little to no sunlight consider supplementation.

Vitamin E (Alpha Tocopherol)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Antioxident
  • Prevents Oxidation
  • Protects Fatty Acids within Cell Membranes
  • Red Blood Cell Formation
  • Prostanoid Synthesis

Prostanoid compounds play an important role in the reproductive process, blood platelet aggregation, energy metabolism, synthesis of DNA and RNA, aging, and prevention of Heart Disease .

Important Fact

Vitamin E guards cell membranes from oxidation, prevents blood clots, engages in cellular respiration, aids and minimizes vitamin deficiency in premature or low-birth-weight infants.

Additional Facts

For athletes in high altitudes (5,000 feet or more above sea level) increased amounts of Vitamin E can help prevent muscle cramps. More than normal levels of Vitamine E can help speed up recovery from injury, or surgery.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

There has been no observation of deficiency symptoms in humans, for the exception of infants, or premature infants. Listed below.

  • Hemolytic Anemia (In premature infants with Vitamin E deficiency)
  • Dermatitis (In premature infants with Vitamin E deficiency)
  • Weakness (In Infants)
  • Red Blood Cell Rupture (In Infants)
  • Fatty Deposits in Muscle Tissue (In Infants)

Overdose Symptoms

  • Vitamine K Interference
  • Anticoagulent Dysfunctions
  • Delayed Blood Clotting

Utilization of 1,000mcg or more is considered overdose.

Great Food Sources

  • Vegetable Oils
  • Margarine
  • Wheat Germ
  • Nuts
  • Meat
  • Animal Fats

Common Supplements

  • D-Alpha Tocopheryl Succinate (Natural)
  • Alpha-Tocopherol ( Bioactive )
  • Alpha-Tocopheryl Acetate ( Bioactive )

Bioactive is the process of having a biological effect.

Vitamin K (Phylloquinone, Menaquinone, Menadione)

  • Phylloquinone (Vitamin K1) develops naturally in green plants.
  • Menaquinone (Vitamin K2) is assembled in the intestines, biproduct of bacterial metabolism.
  • Menadione (Vitamin K3) is the synthetic form of Vitamin K , twice as potent.

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Coenzyme
  • Encourages the formation of Prothrombin.
  • Encourages the formation of Procoagulants.
  • Essential for blood Clotting and Coagulation
  • Maintnance of bone tissue.

Coenzyme is a nonprotein compund and it is a requisite for the functioning of an enzyme in the body.

Important Fact

The main, and most natural sources of Vitamin K are green leafy vegetables. Deficiency of Vitamine K can occur if green leafy vegetables are restricted from your diet.

Additional Facts

Overconsumption of Aspirin can interrupt vitamin-K assisted metabolic pathways, placing a hault in normal blood clotting.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Degrades Prothrombin Levels
  • Amplifies the risk of Hemorrhage .

Overdose Symptoms

Symptoms for verconsumption of Vitamin K have only been seen in infants. See below.

  • Generates Hemolytic Anemia (Seperation of hemoglobin from red blood cells)

Great Food Sources

  • Eggs
  • Cereals
  • Fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Meat
  • Dairy Products

Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Presents it self as a Coenzyme and Cofactor .
  • Involved in the creation, and maintnance of Collagen .
  • Cultivates healthy capillaries, gums, and teeth.
  • Helps with absorption, transport, and storage of Iron
  • Assists in wound healing.
  • Helps metabolize Tyrosine and Phenylalanine .
  • Amplifies Immune Function
  • Shields cells from Free Radical damage.

Collagen is a protein, and it is a vital building block of skin, ligaments, and bones.

Important Fact

Studies have shown that Vitamin C may contribute to muscular strength, reduction of lactate blood levels, and saving glycogen .

Glycogen is a stored form of carbohydrates, used for muscular movement.

Additional Facts

The most natural form of Vitamin C is derived from Rose Hips .

Rose Hips is a plant seed located beneath the petals of a rose.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • May lead to Scurvy
  • Capillary Hemorrhaging

Scurvy is a rare disease found in infants and elderly, not common among adults, characterized by weakning of connective tissue.

Overdose Symptoms

The following symptoms have been observed in individuals who consume more than 2 grams of Vitamin C , within a 24hr period.

Although, excessive amounts of Vitamine C can be used to prevent, or treat the common cold. Elongated periods of megadosing can be harmful, and risky.

  • Headache
  • Increased Urination
  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea

Vitamin B1 (Thiamin)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Transformed into Coenzymes
  • Assists in carbohydrate breakdown
  • Assists in branched-chain amino acid breakdown
  • Production of Ribose

Ribose is a key ingredient used for Nucleic Acid (RNA and DNA) formation.

Nucleic Acids used for normal growth, cellular function, and the encouragement of appetite.

Important Fact

Studies show that Vitamin B1 megadosing, between 3 to 5 days before competition, may enhance performance. Although, further research is required.

Additional Facts

Vitamin B1 deficiency can be activated by unbridled alcohol consumption.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Carbohydrate metabolism irregularity
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Constipation
  • Depression
  • Confusion
  • Poor Coordination
  • Beriberi

Beriberi is a disease that affects the heart and circulatory system, this disease can lead to heart failure.

Overdose Symptoms

Vitamin B1 overdose is rare, since any excessive amounts are cleared by our kidneys.

Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Coenzyme
  • Operates as two Coenzymes
  • Elaborates in Energy Production
  • Elaborates in Cellular Respiration
  • Promotes healthy skin, eyes, and nerves
  • Helps with Tissue Formation
  • Recomended for the maintnance of Red Blood Cells
  • Helps with Iron Metabolism

The two Coenzymes are as follows: Flavin Mononucleotide, and Flavin Adenine Dinucleotide. These two coenzymes are involved with energy production from carbohydrates, fatty acids, and some amino acids.

Important Fact

Studies show that Vitamin B1 megadosing, between 3 to 5 days before competition, may enhance performance. Although, further research is required.

Additional Facts

Vitamin B2 is required for the functioning of both, Vitamin B6 and Niacin.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Inflamed Lips
  • Cracked and Dry Skin
  • Growth Reduction
  • Hair Loss
  • Cataracts
  • Depression
  • Moodiness
  • Nervousness
  • Irritability
  • Generalized Seborrheic Dermatitis

Generalized Seborrheic Dermatitis commonly reffered to as dandruff, is a populosquamous disorder located on the sebum-rich areas of the scalp, face, and trunk.

Overdose Symptoms

Vitamin B2 overdose is unclear, and has not been identified. Despite the fact that, uncontrollable doses of Vitamin B2 can cause liver damage.

Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Active as two important Coenzymes
  • Energy Production
  • Glycolysis Production
  • Carbohydrate Metabolism
  • Protein Metabolism
  • Fatty Acid Synthesis
  • Steroid Synthesis
  • Reduces Cholesterol levels in blood
  • Reduces Fatty Acid levels in blood

The two Coenzymes are as follows: Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide (NAD), and Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide Phosphate (NADP). These two coenzymes are found in all cells, and function in all the metabolic processes mentioned above.

Important Fact

Studies show that Vitamin B3 megadosing, can be determental to athletic performance. This is true, expecially for endurance athletes, since endurance athletes rely on the release of fatty acids for continued performance. Excessive amounts of Vitamin B3 has been shown to block adipose tissue from releasing fatty acids.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Depression
  • Confusion
  • Headaches
  • Elevated Body Fats
  • Fatigue
  • Pellagra

Pellagra is a disease that can be portrayed as dermatitis, inflammation of mucus membranes, dementia, and inflammed and discolored skin.

Overdose Symptoms

Vitamin B3 overdose symptoms can be seen when intake exceeds 50mg, the daily recomended dosage is 35mg. Symptoms and conditions include:

  • Can cause blood capillaries to dilate
  • Skin Flushing
  • Red Skin
  • Itching
  • Warmth

Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Engaged in the metabolism of Amino Acids
  • Engaged in the metabolism of Glycogen
  • Engaged in the metabolism of Sphingoid Bases
  • Engaged in the conversion of Linoleic Acid into Arachidonic Acid
  • Glycogen Breakdown
  • Production of energy
  • Formation of Tissue
  • Red Blood Cell Synthesis

Sphingoid Bases is defined as Lipid Signaling , found in animals and plants.

Important Fact

Do to Vitamin B6 role in amino acid metabolism, intake increase is highly recomended with protein increase.

Additional Facts

Anaerobic athelets may benefit from a slight increase of Vitamin B6, due to its glycogen-liberation function. Glycogen is the primary source of energy for anaerobic exercises like weight lifting, it is especially recommended for strength athletes. Studies have also shown an increse in exercise-induced growth hormone.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Deppression
  • Skin Problems
  • Poor Wound Healing
  • Anemia
  • Fatigue
  • Peripheral Neuropathy
  • Convulsive Seizures

Peripheral Neuropathy happens when the nerves outside of the brain and spinal cord get damaged, this can cause weakness, numbness, and pain more frequently felt at the hands, or feet.

Overdose Symptoms

Excessive amounts of 1,000 mg or more can cause the following symptoms.

  • Skin Lesions
  • Gastrointestinal Misery
  • Shortage of Muscle Control
  • Numbness
  • Light Sensitivity

Vitamin B9 (Folate)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Contributes to Amino Acid metabolism
  • Contributes to Nucleic Acid Synthesis
  • A necessary cofactor in DNA and RNA formation
  • A necessary cofactor in Protein Synthesis
  • A necessary cofactor in Cell Division
  • Stimulates Red Bolld Cell Formation

Vitamin B9 influences rapidly growing tissues, such as the skin, lining of the gastrointestinal tract, bone marrow, and muscle tissue regeneration.

Important Fact

Escalated levels of Vitamin B9 intake has been show to reduce premature birth and birth defects during pregnancy. It supports early development of the fetal brain and spinal cord.

Additional Facts

Including an additional daily intake of 200 mcg can help with muscle tissue repair, ensuring a speedier recovery, especially from exercise enduced stress.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

  • Anemia
  • Birth Defects
  • Sore Tongue
  • Digestive Problems
  • Growth Problems
  • Fatigue
  • Poor Memory
  • Megaloblastic Anemia

Megaloblastic Anemia , a situation in which the bone marrow produces unexpectedly large, structurally abnormal, immature red blood cells.

Overdose Symptoms

Excessive amounts of 1,000 mg or more may cause liver damage and stimulate seizures in anyone with epilepsy.

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Benefits/Roles Include

  • Necessary for...
  • Energy
  • Neural Tissue Development
  • Neural Tissue Function
  • folate Metabolism
  • DNA Synthesis
  • Cell Growth
  • Transformation of Homocysteine to Methionine
  • Immune System Function
  • Red Blood Cell Synthesis

Important Fact

Female athelets may benefit from an increas in Vitamin B12 . Research also indicates that atheletes who suffer from poor absorption may see an incline in high-intensity performance.

Additional Facts

Vegans may need to supplement, foods rich in Vitamin B12 are only found in animal products, such as Meats.

Defficient? Here are some common symptoms.

Defficiency symptoms are not common and may be caused by poor absorption rather than intake.

  • Pernicious Anemia
  • Fatigue
  • Irritability
  • Loss of appetite
  • Constipation
  • Headache
  • Sore Tongue

Pernicious Anemia is a disease that develops from restrained absorption of Vitamin B12.

Overdose Symptoms

Overdose symptoms, or damage due to excessive Vitamin B12 intake has not been documented, or recorded. Although, if a physician notices high levels of B12 in the blood, this can be an indication of a diseased liver, or poor-functioning kidney.
